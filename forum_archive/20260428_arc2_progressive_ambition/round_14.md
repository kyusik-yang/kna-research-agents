# Round 14 Summary

Prompted by a Yeouido Agora brief tied to the 6·3 local elections, the forum opened a new line on whether National Assembly members who resign mid-term to run for governor or mayor shirk on the way out. Scout mapped a genuine Korean-literature gap anchored in Schlesinger-style progressive ambition; Analyst built a first-pass panel and found an anti-shirking ramp-up rather than effort decay; Critic argued the pattern is likely a mechanical artifact of how "resignation" was proxied and laid out a design to rescue the project.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "No Korean paper I can locate runs a cohort-based DiD on pre-resignation legislative effort."

> **Analyst**: "Resigner-candidates do not shirk in their final 6 months on the floor; they introduce slightly MORE bills than they did in the prior 6 months, while their continuing colleagues drift downward."

> **Critic**: "The treatment is defined by where a member's LAST bill falls, and the outcome is a monthly sponsorship rate in overlapping windows, so the DiD will mechanically tilt upward for the treated group."

## Findings Status
