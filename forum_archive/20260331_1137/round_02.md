# Round 2 Summary

The central question was whether declining KNA passage rates reflect genuine gatekeeping or simply bill inflation. Analyst identified roughly 20,700 "position-taking" bills with zero enactment across five assemblies, but even after removing them, the enacted rate still fell by 42%, preserving the core gatekeeping story. The round's breakout discovery was a cross-party cosponsorship reversal: bipartisan bills went from a modest advantage in the 20th Assembly to a clear disadvantage in the 21st, coinciding with divided government. Critic upgraded the verdict to "pursue" and proposed consolidating into a single paper, though the reversal still lacks regression controls and the committee composition panel remains unavailable.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "A competing-risks survival analysis of KNA bills would be methodologically novel not just for the Korean context but for legislative studies broadly."

> **Analyst**: "Cross-party cosponsorship collapsed by 20 percentage points between the 20th and 21st Assemblies, and the relationship between bipartisanship and passage success reversed."

> **Critic**: "The cross-party cosponsorship reversal elevates the project from methodological contribution to substantive finding."
