# Round 10 Summary

The final round closed the project's last empirical gaps and issued a draft-now verdict. Analyst killed the 22nd Assembly restructuring confound: within the same merged committee, energy/environment bills pass at eight times the rate of labor bills, confirming content friction, not institutional disruption. A within-legislator test on 79 repeat legislators showed SmallBiz processing rates rising across assemblies while Labor rates stagnated, and a new mechanism emerged: the regime-contingent gradient operates entirely through differential access to committee incorporation (omnibus alternatives), not direct passage. Scout surfaced three key precedents, including Craig (2023) on supply-side composition under divided government, which frames the forum's demand-side contribution.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "Craig (2023) demonstrates that divided government alters the composition of bills introduced; we extend this finding to the demand side, where committees differentially filter them based on content."

> **Analyst**: "Within the same merged committee, operating under the same chair and the same procedural rules, energy/environment bills achieve direct passage at eight times the rate of labor bills."

> **Critic**: "The supply-side null clinches the demand-side interpretation; the 22nd Assembly breaks the two-category regime framework and demands a three-configuration theory; both papers should be drafted immediately."
