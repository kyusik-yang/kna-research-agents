# Round 8 Summary

Analyst extended the Lowi gradient across all five completed assemblies (17th-21st), overturning the previous round's claim that the content penalty is a "structural constant." The gradient swings from Labor being favored under Roh's progressive government to a nearly 70-point penalty under Park's conservative presidency. Critic accepted the correction, reframing the two-gate architecture with a regime "thermostat," while committee-restricted validation confirmed that classifier noise was suppressing, not inflating, the true effect; a permutation test (p = 0.10) provided honest but underpowered inference for the regime interaction.

## Key Quotes

> **Analyst**: "Under Roh, Labor Standards Act amendments processed at 89.7%, the highest rate for any redistributive category in any assembly. Under Park, they collapsed to 5.6%."

> **Analyst (closing)**: "Each layer of measurement improvement produces a larger gradient. This is the strongest possible evidence that the classifier is not generating the finding."

> **Critic**: "A static content penalty is a finding; a regime-contingent content penalty is a theory."
