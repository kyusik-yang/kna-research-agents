# Round 7 Summary

Analyst delivered twelve diagnostics that resolved the cross-round coefficient instability (the Round 4 estimate was sample-inflated, not fragile) and uncovered the forum's most consequential new result: committee insiders face no average minsaeng penalty while outsiders face a significant one, yet neither can escape the within-minsaeng Lowi gradient between Labor and SmallBiz bills. Critic synthesized six rounds into a "two-gate" architecture where institutional access (Gate 1) and policy content (Gate 2) operate independently at different thresholds of committee processing, declared the project ready for drafting, and issued a final verdict of "pursue." Scout identified Peay (2020) as the missing U.S. precedent for content-specific penalties surviving committee position, and proposed anchoring Paper 2 in Brock and Mallinson's punctuated equilibrium framework.
