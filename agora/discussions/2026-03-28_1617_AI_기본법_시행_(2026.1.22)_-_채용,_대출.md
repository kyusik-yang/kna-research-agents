## Moderator Report

## Yeouido Agora Discussion Report: AI Basic Law

**1. Stimulus**
Korea's AI Basic Law (effective 2026.1.22) designates 10 "high-impact AI" sectors with safety evaluation, transparency, and watermarking obligations, drawing simultaneous criticism from startups (compliance burden) and civil society (insufficient regulation).

**2. Sentiment Overview**
Citizens broadly acknowledged the necessity of AI regulation but expressed deep skepticism about implementation readiness. The dominant sentiment was frustration that the law was enacted before substantive guidelines existed. Notably, neither pro-regulation nor pro-industry participants were satisfied - a pattern several citizens themselves identified as potentially indicating a rough equilibrium.

**3. Key Themes**

- **Regulatory ambiguity as the core problem**: "안전성 평가 기준이 뭔지도 아직 제대로 안 나왔는데 시행부터 하겠다는 게 ㄹㅇ 한국식 규제" (Han Dongwook, 29, Busan). Multiple participants noted that subordinate legislation remains unwritten.
- **Asymmetric compliance burden**: "대기업이야 전담팀 꾸리면 되지만 우리 같은 제조업 중소는 그냥 AI 안 쓰게 되는 거예요" (Oh Sangmin, 48, Suwon). The absence of SME exemptions or tiered obligations was a recurring concern.
- **Absence of prohibition categories**: Kang Soyeon and Yang Heejin independently flagged that unlike the EU AI Act, the Korean law contains zero banned AI applications - a structural gap with constitutional implications.
- **Invisible populations**: Ahn Suji raised children's biometric data; Moon Changho noted rural irrelevance; Yoon Misook highlighted elderly patients unable to comprehend AI-assisted diagnoses.

**4. Demographic Patterns**
Age and region mattered more than ideology. Older rural participants (Moon, Kwon) framed AI as irrelevant to their lives. Urban professionals (Kang, Yang, Song) engaged with comparative legal frameworks. Working parents and healthcare workers focused on vulnerable populations rather than industry competitiveness.

**5. Communication Gaps**
Citizens lacked clarity on which specific applications fall under "high-impact AI," what the safety evaluation entails, and whether any support measures accompany the mandates.

**6. Top Research Demands**
1. What share of elderly patients understand AI involvement in their diagnoses, and does informed consent function as protection or liability shield? (Yoon Misook)
2. How do childcare facilities handle children's biometric data, and does any baseline survey exist? (Ahn Suji)
3. How does the AI Basic Law's budget allocation compare between large-firm support and SME compliance assistance? (Kwon Taesung)

**7. Suggested Research Topic**
`--topic "Compliance capacity gap: how Korea's AI Basic Law's uniform obligations differentially burden firms by size, sector, and region, compared with the EU AI Act's tiered exemption framework"`
