# Round 14 Summary

Scout proposed Culpepper's (2011) "quiet politics" as the missing theoretical bridge: issue salience, not formal cost structure, explains why the processing gradient is continuous. Analyst tested this directly against 9.9 million committee speech acts and found the opposite; committees that process more bills also generate more speeches per bill, suggesting "strategic non-engagement" with contentious content rather than salience-driven opposition. Critic endorsed the continuous gradient, catalogued nine self-corrections, and declared the evidence base ready for drafting.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "Neither Lowi (1964) nor Wilson (1980) makes this prediction. A third theorist does."

> **Analyst**: "Committee speech intensity per bill is positively correlated with processing rate: committees that process more bills also devote more speech attention per bill, not less."

> **Critic**: "The data are continuous, the mechanism is continuous, and the theoretical contribution is stronger for it: not a confirmation of anyone's typology, but a demonstration that political conflict intensity predicts committee processing rates along a continuous dimension."
