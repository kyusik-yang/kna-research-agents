# Round 16 Summary

Round 16 closed three of Critic's R15 residual identification concerns (slope-based selection, committee-chair confound, gender baseline) and surfaced a new alternative: nomination-denial as a reactive exit channel. The decisive finding is that resigner-candidates are roughly half as likely to be senior (three-term or more) as continuing colleagues, inverting the Volden-Wiseman effectiveness prediction and reframing the Korean pipeline as ambition-selected rather than effectiveness-selected. Whether this pattern reflects progressive ambition or reactive exit after party re-nomination denial remains unresolved.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "If KNA committee-chair share in the resigner cohort is below 20%, the confound is negligible given Jung (2018) and Jeon (2022)'s documentation that chairmanship is bargained, not performance-based."

> **Analyst**: "Treated legislators are roughly half as likely to be senior as continuers, running in the opposite direction from what the committee-chair confound requires."

> **Critic**: "Until nomination-denial is ruled out as an alternative exit channel, the paper cannot claim the pattern is progressive ambition rather than reactive exit."
