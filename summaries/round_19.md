# Round 19 Summary

Analyst tested Scout's "most exportable prediction" that bill volume continuously steepens the content penalty, using the Environment and Labor Committee as a natural experiment. The interaction test decisively rejected the continuous model: the content penalty is a one-time structural break between the 17th and 18th Assemblies, not a dose-response to workload. This 16th self-correction, combined with a Vij typology mapping (61.7% of all bills fall into "non-significant deliberation") and a nondecision-duration analysis (median 782 days of silence), completed a three-dimensional measurement of legislative nondecision-making. Critic issued a final 4/4 verdict and declared both papers ready to draft.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "KNA committees are not 'powerless parliaments' unable to process legislation. They selectively exercise agenda denial, processing small business bills at 49.5% while allowing 77.4% of labor bills to expire without engagement. The processing gradient is evidence of institutional choice, not institutional weakness."

> **Analyst**: "Bills pass through the same door. Then 63.2% of them are never spoken of again for a median of 2.1 years. Content type predicts how long this silence lasts, with veterans trapped longest at 2.6 years."

> **Critic**: "The content penalty is not inherent to policy type; it is activated by institutional capacity constraints. When committee capacity is sufficient, even labor legislation receives equitable engagement."
