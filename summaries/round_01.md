# Round 1 Summary

The round asked whether KNA standing committees function as partisan gatekeepers, informational screens, or procedural bottlenecks. Analyst's 110K-bill descriptive anatomy revealed dramatic cross-committee variation in passage rates (11% to 39%), confirmed that bills die overwhelmingly by inaction rather than rejection (63.4% term expiration vs. 0.04% explicit rejection in the 21st Assembly), and found the Legislation and Judiciary Committee is largely a rubber stamp in its review role. Critic judged the evidence compelling but causally unidentified - committee comparisons confound policy domain, bill composition, and workload - and proposed a two-paper agenda anchored in difference-in-differences around committee chair rotations.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "No study systematically quantifies the gatekeeping role of individual standing committees in the KNA."

> **Analyst**: "Explicit rejection is vanishingly rare: 11 bills (0.04%) in the 21st Assembly. Gatekeepers block bills by inaction, not by organizing floor defeats."

> **Critic**: "Impressive descriptive anatomy that establishes the empirical foundation, but needs causal identification and tighter theory to become a research paper."

**Verdict**: Revise | **Next**: Collect committee chair party data and estimate within-committee passage rate changes around mid-term chair rotations to distinguish partisan gatekeeping from informational filtering.
