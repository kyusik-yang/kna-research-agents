# Round 1 Summary

## Topic
Legislative polarization and committee gatekeeping in the Korean National Assembly (17th-22nd).

## Key Findings

**Scout** mapped the literature landscape, identifying 20+ publications across international and Korean journals. Three specific research gaps emerged: (1) no Korean Legislative Effectiveness Scores exist despite available data, (2) the LJC's quantitative "kill rate" has never been measured, and (3) committee composition effects on bill outcomes remain untested in Korea.

**Analyst** queried the KNA database and produced a surprising result: the Legislation and Judiciary Committee (법사위) is *not* the primary bottleneck. Its block rate is only 8-15% of committee-passed bills. The real gatekeeper is the standing committee chair, who bundles member bills into committee alternatives (대안반영폐기). The absorption ratio tripled from 1.9:1 (17th Assembly) to 6.5:1 (22nd Assembly). Meanwhile, inter-bloc polarization increased 61% from the 20th to 22nd Assembly, with within-party heterogeneity collapsing simultaneously.

**Critic** rated the findings (2) promising with caveats, identifying three threats: anticipated reactions bias (the LJC's "shadow power" may suppress bills upstream), roll-call selection bias inflating polarization estimates, and missing theoretical mechanisms linking polarization to rising absorption ratios. Three competing hypotheses were proposed (credit-dilution, chair empowerment, complexity management) along with concrete identification strategies (committee chair turnover DiD, 22nd Assembly impeachment crisis as natural experiment, provision-level text analysis).

## Consensus
All three agents agree the absorption mechanism (대안반영폐기) is under-studied and represents a genuinely novel research direction.

## Open Questions
- Does LJC exercise "shadow power" through anticipated reactions?
- Is the rising absorption ratio driven by bill inflation, chair empowerment, or policy complexity?
- Can provision-level text matching reveal partisan content filtering within committee alternatives?

## Proposed Research Direction
"Who Controls the Bundle? Committee Chairs, Partisan Sorting, and Legislative Absorption in the Korean National Assembly" - rated as strong research direction (3) if causal identification succeeds.
