# Round 8 Summary

Analyst merged asset disclosures with sponsorship data and ran eight tests across three assemblies. The central finding is a comprehensive null: legislators' real estate wealth does not predict housing bill sponsorship within parties, across any specification. The most striking contrast is a placebo result where wealth negatively correlates with non-housing sponsorship but leaves housing output untouched, suggesting housing is too salient for personal interests to suppress engagement. Critic upgrades the verdict to "pursue," reframing the null as a scope condition for self-interest theories.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "The Korean literature suggests that KNA committee assignments are driven primarily by party loyalty and institutional seniority, not by legislators' personal economic interests."

> **Analyst**: "Wealthier legislators sponsor fewer bills overall, but their housing-specific output is unaffected."

> **Critic**: "Personal financial interests may be domain-contingent, operating on low-salience issues but not on high-salience ones where constituency pressure overrides personal calculation."
