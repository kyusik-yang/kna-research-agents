# Round 12 Summary

The forum asked whether legislators' pre-legislative careers (prosecutor, journalist, academic) predict distinct questioning styles in Korea's 7.4M committee Q&A dyads. Analyst's first empirical test, based on 21 hand-coded legislators, found that former prosecutors carry elevated legal vocabulary into non-judiciary committees (about 50% above baseline), but committee identity dwarfs any career effect. Critic judged the finding promising but trapped inside three compounding identification problems: endogenous committee assignment, an unvalidated keyword classifier, and a sample too small for causal claims.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "No study has used large-scale Q&A transcript data to test whether pre-legislative professional background predicts questioning style, questioning effectiveness, or oversight quality in committee hearings."

> **Analyst**: "The 'career persistence' effect is legislator-specific, not career-category-universal; 전주혜 and 김도읍 retain elevated legal language even outside judiciary at roughly 2.5x baseline, while 최강욱 and 주호영 converge completely."

> **Critic**: "A genuine literature gap is undermined by three compounding identification problems, and finding that prosecutors use legal vocabulary more often is not evidence that their questioning style is different; it is evidence that their word choices differ."

## Findings Status

| Finding | Status |
|---------|--------|
| Committee identity shapes questioning style far more than individual career background | preliminary |
| Former prosecutors retain legal vocabulary on non-judiciary committees, about 50% above baseline | preliminary |
| Career persistence varies by individual legislator, not uniformly by career category | preliminary |
| Ruling-party and opposition legislators show nearly identical confrontation rates | preliminary |
| Seniority produces a U-shaped pattern in committee concentration | preliminary |

**Verdict**: Revise | **Next**: Round 13 should validate the keyword classifier (report overlap rates, add structural question features) and test the committee-switcher design using legislators who changed committees across assemblies.
