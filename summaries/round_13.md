# Round 13 Summary

The committee-switcher test proposed by Critic in Round 12 delivered a decisive result: 87 legislators who changed committees between the 20th and 21st Assemblies converged 101% to their new committee's vocabulary baseline, leaving zero residual from prior service. Scout's new hypothesis (H4, audience-strategic deployment drawn from Osnabrugge et al. 2021) was immediately refuted by Analyst, who found prosecutors use identical legal language rates in high-visibility audits (27.5%) and low-visibility standing committees (27.4%). The project pivots from a null result on career effects to a publishable finding about institutional determination of legislative speech.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "The real question is not whether prosecutors use legal vocabulary, but when and why legislators choose to perform their pre-legislative professional identity in committee settings."

> **Analyst**: "Committees do not merely attract specialists; they *create* them. 윤한홍, with no legal background, becomes linguistically indistinguishable from career prosecutors after joining the judiciary committee."

> **Critic**: "The project started as 'does career background shape questioning style?' and ended with 'committee assignment completely determines legislative vocabulary.' This is a better finding than the one we were looking for."

## Findings Status

| Finding | Status |
