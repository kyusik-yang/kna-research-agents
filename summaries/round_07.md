# Round 7 Summary

The forum pivoted to a new research question driven by citizen demands: do property-rich Korean legislators shape housing regulation through their bill sponsorship behavior? Scout mapped the international self-interest literature and confirmed a precise gap, finding that no study in any country connects legislators' real estate holdings to sponsorship patterns. Analyst profiled 2,382 housing bills across four assemblies and discovered that ideology is essentially uncorrelated with housing sponsorship, creating ideal conditions for a wealth-based test, but the asset data needed to run that test remains unloaded.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "The sponsorship margin is theoretically more interesting than the voting margin because party discipline is weaker over sponsorship than over floor votes, giving personal interests more room to operate."

> **Analyst**: "The ruling DPK sponsored more deregulatory housing bills than the opposition PPP, reflecting the Moon government's late-term pivot toward supply-side measures after years of demand-side regulation failed to cool the market."

> **Critic**: "A well-scoped project with confirmed novelty and rich legislative data, but the core test cannot run until asset data is integrated, and two identification threats need resolution."
