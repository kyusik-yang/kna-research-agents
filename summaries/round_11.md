# Round 11 Summary

Scout predicted that opposition committee chairs in the 22nd Assembly should block ruling-party bills at elevated rates, following Cox-McCubbins negative agenda control theory. Analyst tested this with 110K+ bills across six assemblies and found the opposite: ruling-party bills enjoy a consistent 3-4 percentage-point passage advantage regardless of which party holds the chairs. The real mechanism is constructive bundling, where chairs consolidate multiple bills into omnibus alternatives (위원장 대안) that pass at 99.7%, a finding Critic endorsed as genuinely novel and publishable.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "No study, in any country, has measured the causal effect of committee chair partisanship on the rate of bill absorption, controlling for bill characteristics."

> **Analyst**: "Each committee chair omnibus bill in the 22nd Assembly consolidates the content of nearly five legislator bills. This is the chair's real power: not deciding which bills die, but deciding which bills' content gets incorporated."

> **Critic**: "The alternative reading of Analyst's findings is not that chairs exercise a different kind of power but that they exercise no independent power at all; the chair produces an omnibus bill that reflects an inter-party agreement, and the plenary rubber-stamps it."
