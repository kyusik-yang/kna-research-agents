# Round 10 Summary

The forum's final round resolved its last major causal ambiguity: the Lowi gradient is demand-side, not supply-side. Analyst showed that Labor and SmallBiz bills are indistinguishable on observable quality (text length, cosponsor count), yet committees filter them dramatically differently. The 22nd Assembly, where progressive DPK chairs control all committees under conservative President Yoon, produced the lowest-ever Labor pass rate (2.3%), breaking the "regime thermostat" metaphor from Round 8 and forcing Critic to propose a three-configuration theory connecting Lowi to Cameron's veto bargaining and Krehbiel's pivotal politics. Hearings data revealed a surprising decoupling: conservative ruling-party legislators questioned labor officials more intensely while blocking more labor bills. Critic's final verdict: both papers are draftable now.
