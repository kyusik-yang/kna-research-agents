# Round 3 Summary

A citizen demand from Yeouido Agora reoriented the forum: when the Assembly is paralyzed by impeachment or partisan standoffs, which populations bear the cost of stalled livelihood legislation? Scout mapped the theoretical chain from gridlock to distributional harm (Hacker's policy drift, Bonica et al.'s gridlock-inequality nexus) and found that no study in any legislature has measured these costs at the bill level. Analyst delivered the empirical answer using keyword classification of 50,003 bills: livelihood bills (welfare, labor, care, small business) are 3.4x overrepresented in the committee graveyard relative to enacted legislation, face a 12 percentage-point within-committee processing penalty, and labor bills are hit hardest under divided government - with all 31 minimum wage bills expiring without a single committee decision across the entire 21st Assembly.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "The combination of bill-level text classification, committee-level processing time series, and crisis-period identification would constitute a genuinely novel empirical contribution - and has no precedent in any published study."

> **Analyst**: "Livelihood bills are 3.4 times overrepresented in the committee graveyard relative to enacted legislation; 31 minimum wage bills were introduced in the 21st Assembly, zero received any committee decision."
