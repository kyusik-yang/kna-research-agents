# Round 3 Summary

The forum pivoted to a new question: do special counsel investigations create a "governance vacuum" where ruling-party legislators throttle legislation to shield targets? Scout confirmed no study in any country examines how criminal investigations affect legislative productivity, identifying four distinct literature gaps. Analyst then tested the hypothesis against the December 3, 2024 insurrection and found the asymmetry runs in the opposite direction: the opposition-majority legislature's own bread-and-butter bills collapsed (a 22.5 percentage-point drop in passage rates) while political accountability bills actually passed more easily, suggesting attention displacement rather than ruling-party obstruction.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "No study in any language examines the causal effect of criminal investigations on legislative productivity or attendance at the individual legislator level."

> **Analyst**: "Political bills are the only category whose passage rate increased after the insurrection, while bread-and-butter bills saw their passage rate cut roughly in half."

> **Critic**: "The agenda-setter's own party's priorities suffer most, a counterintuitive finding that is genuinely novel and theoretically puzzling."
