# Round 5 Summary

Scout mapped the literature on gender and legislative behavior in the Korean National Assembly, identifying five research gaps, the most important being the confound between gender and mandate type (PR vs. SMD). Analyst's eight empirical findings revealed a striking reversal: women who won district (SMD) seats in the 21st and 22nd Assemblies now achieve higher bill passage rates than any other group, flipping the earlier pattern where PR women led. Critic flagged that the central literature gap was overstated (Kweon and Ryan 2021 already studied gender x mandate type for sponsorship) and warned the reversal may be a governing-party incumbency artifact, demanding within-party decomposition before the finding can be trusted.

## Key Quotes

> **Scout**: "The behavioral effects of 'being a woman legislator' are thoroughly confounded with the behavioral effects of 'being a PR legislator.'"

> **Analyst**: "Women SMD legislators in the 21st and 22nd Assemblies achieved higher passage rates than any other group."

> **Critic**: "The 'SMD women advantage' may be nothing more than 'governing-party women advantage,' which is theoretically uninteresting."

## Findings Status

| Finding | Status |
|---------|--------|
